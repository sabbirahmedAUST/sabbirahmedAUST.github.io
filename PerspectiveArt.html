Insight / Incite

This project presents data in a participatory way. Users actively interpret structured information, form their own mental image, and then compare it with the revealed image.

Setup

Initially the artwork is fully covered. Participants do not know what the image is. Instructions and response cards are placed on the table.

Split Perception

The class is divided into two groups. Group A sees the image and describes it using words. Group B sees only words and imagines the image.

Response

The cover is removed. Participants view the artwork, write responses, and pin them.

Results

Even though the full data was not provided, key words and their arrangement helped participants visualize the image. Most imagined something related to the ocean and a boat.

Complex Approach

The first artwork was easy and direct. The second version is more complex, with fewer clear words and less direct guidance.

Participants now see only words, imagine the image, and doodle it.

Final Results

Even with fewer direct words and drawing, participants still remembered key ideas and almost guessed the image correctly.
